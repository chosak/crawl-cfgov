Emergency Rental Assistance resources

This shareable content is designed to help you spread important information about the CFPB’s tools and resources related to housing insecurity during the coronavirus pandemic.

Jump to:

  • Social Media
  • Videos
  • Images
  • Emails
  • Handouts

How to use this toolkit

You can insert the content pieces below into newsletters, blogs, email blasts, and social media posts to help get the word out about these resources. When sharing this content, please indicate that it was sourced from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

If you have any questions about these materials or how to use them, please contact Media Relations.

Emails (English)

Sample Emergency Rental Assistance email to renters and landlords

Goal: To promote the Rental Assistance Finder tool and get consumers to use it to search for rental assistance in their area

Subject: Get help covering rent, utilities, or moving costs

By: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

If you’re having trouble making rent payments, you’re not alone. The COVID-19 pandemic has made it difficult for renters and landlords to cover housing costs. Many renters are worried about catching up on past-due rent and facing eviction. Help is available.

As a renter—or a landlord—you can apply for money from the U.S. Department of Treasury’s Emergency Rental Assistance (ERA) program. The funds are being distributed by state and local organizations to their local communities. This money can help with rent, utility bills, and even moving costs.

Use our Rental Assistance Finder to search for a program in your community.

Sample Emergency Rental Assistance email to practitioners and intermediaries

Goal: To ask practitioners to share the Rental Assistance Finder tool with their networks

Subject: Help is available for people struggling with housing costs

By: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

The COVID-19 pandemic has made it difficult for renters and landlords to cover housing costs. Many renters are worried about catching up on past-due rent and facing eviction. Help is available.

Renters—and landlords—can apply for money from the U.S. Department of Treasury’s Emergency Rental Assistance (ERA) program. The funds are being distributed by state and local organizations to their local communities. This money can help with rent, utility bills, and even moving costs.

Our new Rental Assistance Finder allows people to find aid based on the state or territory or tribe or tribal lands where they live. Please share this resource with your networks so that more people can stay in their homes and avoid eviction.

Emails (Spanish)

Sample Emergency Rental Assistance email to renters and landlords

Goal: To promote the Rental Assistance Finder tool and get consumers to use it to search for rental assistance in their area

Subject: Encuentre ayuda con la renta, los servicios básicos y los costos de mudanza

Por la Oficina para la Protección Financiera del Consumidor

Si tiene problemas pagando su renta, sepa que no está solo. La pandemia del COVID-19 ha hecho difícil para inquilinos y caseros el cubrir los costos de vivienda. Muchos inquilinos temen ser alcanzados por meses de renta vencida y tener que enfrentar un desalojo. Hay ayuda disponible.

Sea usted inquilino, o casero, puede solicitar dinero del Programa de Asistencia de Emergencia con la Renta del Departamento del Tesoro (ERA, siglas en inglés), el cual le ayuda con la renta, las facturas de servicios básicos, e inclusive, los costos de una mudanza.

Use nuestra Herramienta de Búsqueda de Asistencia para que encuentre un programa en su comunidad

Sample Emergency Rental Assistance email to practitioners and intermediaries

Goal: To ask practitioners to share the Rental Assistance Finder tool with their networks

Subject: Hay ayuda disponible para la gente que tenga problemas con los costos de vivienda

Por la Oficina para la Protección Financiera del Consumidor

La pandemia del COVID-19 ha hecho difícil para inquilinos y caseros el cubrir los costos de vivienda. Muchos inquilinos temen ser alcanzados por meses de renta vencida y tener que enfrentar un desalojo. Hay ayuda disponible.

Tanto inquilinos, como caseros, pueden solicitar dinero del Programa de Asistencia de Emergencia con la Renta del Departamento del Tesoro (ERA, siglas en inglés). Los fondos están siendo distribuidos por organizaciones estatales y locales, entre las comunidades de sus localidades. Este dinero puede ayudar con la renta, las facturas de servicios básicos, e inclusive, los costos de mudanza.

Nuestra nueva Herramienta de Búsqueda de Asistencia, le permite a la gente encontrar ayuda en el estado, territorio, tribu o tierras tribales donde viva. Por favor, comparta este recurso con los miembros de su red, para colaborar en que más personas permanezcan en sus viviendas y eviten el desalojo.
