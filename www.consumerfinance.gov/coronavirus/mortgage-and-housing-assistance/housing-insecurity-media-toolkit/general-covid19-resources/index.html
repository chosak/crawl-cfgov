General COVID-19 resources

This shareable content is designed to help you spread important information about the CFPB’s tools and resources related to overarching financial support during the coronavirus pandemic.

Coronavirus financial education resources

How to use this toolkit

You can insert the content pieces below into newsletters, blogs, email blasts, and social media posts to help get the word out about these resources. When sharing this content, please indicate that it was sourced from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

If you have any questions about these materials or how to use them, please contact Media Relations.

Images (English)

General Coronavirus Relief

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

If you're working with lenders on payment assistance programs or forbearance, routinely check your credit reports to make sure they are accurate and reflect your agreements.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

Check in via phone call or video call and ask questions if your loved one expresses concerns about money or mentions unusual activity.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

If you don't know who owns or backs your mortgage, call your servicer. To the best of their knowledge they must provide you the name, address, and telephone number of who owns your loan.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

When you secure forbearance or another mortgage relief option, ask your servicer to provide written documentation that confirms the details of your agreement.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

Be wary of charities calling you for money, don't pay anymore for a COVID-19 cure, and don't trust any text or email saying SSA will suspend or decrease your benefits because of COVID-19.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

If you're having trouble paying your bills, contact the companies your owe money to. Discuss your situation and options.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protecting your finances during a pandemic

Most people will receive their Economic Impact Payments automatically. But some non-filers need to take action and submit their information to the IRS.

Learn more at consumerfinance.gov/coronavirus

Protegiendo sus finanzas durante una pandemia

Si no sabe quién es el dueño o quién respalda su hipoteca, llame al administrador de la misma. Éste deberá, a su mejor saber y entender, entregarle el nombre, dirección y teléfono de qui sea el dueño de su préstamo.

Protegiendo sus finanzas durante una pandemia

Cuando haya asegurado recibir un aplazamiento, o alguna otra opción de alivio de hipoteca, pídale al administrador que le entregue documentación por escrito, que confirme los detalles de su acuerdo.

Protegiendo sus finanzas durante una pandemia

Esté alerta con caridades que le llamen pidiéndole dinero, no pague nada más por una cura del COVID-19, y no confíe en ningún mensaje de texto, o email que le diga que el Seguro Social va a suspender o reducir sus beneficios por culpa del COVID-19.

Emails

Sample coronavirus relief email (English)

Subject: CFPB COVID-19 Financial Education Resources

By: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

The CFPB has up-to-date information and resources to help you protect and manage your finances during this difficult time.

  • Guide to coronavirus housing relief options

If you can’t make your rent or mortgage payments because of the coronavirus, start by understanding your options and reaching out for help.

  • Beware of scams related to the coronavirus

Recognize and prevent fraud and scams during the coronavirus pandemic.

  • What you need to know about student loans and the coronavirus pandemic

Federally held student loan payments are postponed and interest has been waived. Learn what that means for your loans.

Visit the CFPB’s website for more tools and resources as the situation continues to change and evolve. We encourage you to share these resources with your friends, families, and others in your networks.

Stay up to date with CFPB's coronavirus resources

Sample coronavirus relief email (Spanish)

Subject: Recursos del CFPB de Educación Financiera contra el COVID-19

Por la Oficina para la Protección Financiera del Consumidor

El CFPB cuenta con información y recursos actualizados para ayudarle a proteger y manejar sus finanzas durante estos tiempos difíciles.

  • Guía de las opciones de alivio habitacional por el coronavirus

Si por causa del coronavirus no puede pagar su renta o su hipoteca, comience a entender sus opciones y a buscar ayuda.

  • Esté alerta con estafas relacionadas con el coronavirus

Sepa cómo reconocer y prevenir el fraude y las estafas durante la pandemia del coronavirus.

  • Lo que necesita saber sobre los préstamos estudiantiles durante la pandemia del coronavirus

Los pagos a préstamos estudiantiles otorgados por el gobierno Federal han sido pospuestos y los intereses perdonados. Sepa qué significa esto para sus préstamos.

Mientras la situación continúa cambiando y evolucionando, encuentre más herramientas y recursos en la página web del CFPB . Le animamos a que comparta estos recursos con familiares, amigos, y conocidos.
