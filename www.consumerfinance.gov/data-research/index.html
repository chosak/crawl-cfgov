Data and research

Find research we’ve published and information we’ve collected about the consumer financial marketplace. Use these resources to improve your understanding of the marketplace.

Our research and reports

Explore our reports on a variety of subjects including financial well-being, consumer complaints, debt collection and lending practices, mortgage origination and servicing, payday lending, consumer experiences and financial education, and more.

  • Browse reports
  • Explore the consumer finance research hub
  • See all peer-reviewed reports

Mortgage performance trends

We publish data on mortgage delinquency rates.  See how trends change over time and across geographies.

  • Explore mortgage performance trends

Consumer finance research hub

Our PhD social scientists design and conduct foundational, policy-relevant research on consumer finance and household behavior.

  • Explore the research hub

Consumer Complaint Database

We send thousands of consumers’ complaints about financial products and services to companies for response. Data from those complaints help us understand the financial marketplace and protect consumers.  

  • Use the Consumer Complaint Database

HMDA data

The Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) requires many financial institutions to maintain, report, and publicly disclose information about mortgages. We’ve made data available in an interactive tool and a public API.  

  • Access HMDA data

Small business lending database

The CFPB’s small business lending database will be the most comprehensive source of publicly available information on U.S. small business lending.

  • Learn about data submission and publication

Credit card agreements and surveys

We maintain and provide access to resources where you can learn more about credit cards, including general credit card agreement terms and conditions and survey data about pricing and fees.

  • See credit card agreements and surveys

Prepaid account agreements

We provide a searchable database of prepaid account agreements, along with resources to help prepaid account issuers submit agreements in compliance with the Prepaid Rule.

  • See prepaid account agreements and submission resources

Student banking and college credit card marketing agreements

We publish annual reports to Congress. We collect information annually from credit card issuers who have marketing agreements with universities, colleges, or affiliated organizations such as alumni associations, sororities, fraternities, and foundations; we maintain publicly accessible files of the agreements.

  • Access annual reports and college credit card marketing agreements

Survey of mortgage originations

We survey consumers who took out a mortgage in the past 18 months or so — including refinancing a mortgage — in order to know about your experience.

  • Respond to a mortgage borrowers survey invitation
  • Respond to a mortgage originations survey invitation
  • Read about mortgage originations survey and results

Financial well-being survey data

We measured the financial well-being of adults in America: their sense of financial security and freedom of choice—both in the present and when considering the future—and other factors that may support financial well-being.

  • Explore the survey data

Nonbank registry

The CFPB’s nonbank registry will provide a list of nonbanks that have registered certain information with the CFPB and is designed to detect and deter corporate offenders that have broken consumer laws and are subject to federal, state, or local government or court orders.

  • Learn more about the nonbank registry

CFPB Researchers

The CFPB's Office of Research undertakes rigorous and innovative research to improve our understanding of consumer behavior, financial service providers, markets and effects of regulation and other public policies in consumer finance.

  • View CFPB Researchers

CFPB Research Conference

Our seventh conference held on May 2–3, 2024, featured research from a range of disciplines and approaches that inform the topic of consumer finance.

  • Learn about the conference

How we handle personally identifiable information

At the CFPB, we have nine privacy principles that guide when and how we collect, use, share, and protect personally identifiable information (PII).
